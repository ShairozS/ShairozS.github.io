Research

Human Activity Recognition using Machine Learning

An application of modern Machine Learning methods to the problem of classifying human movement. Data is collected from a hip-mounted Galaxy S2 smartphone, utilizing the gyroscope and GPS sensors of the device. Data is pre-processed and explored with a variety of linear and non-linear models. Our final model (a Linear Support Vector Classifier) achieves a roughly 96% classification accuracy. Presented at SIAM conference 2015.

The Real State of CO2

A statistical paper that makes updates to the data set of global CO2 emissions presented by Bjorn Lomborg (environmental economist) in his popular book “The Skeptical Environmentalist”and reflects on the validity of his predictions in current times. Correlations are presented with CO2 emissions and over a half-dozen socio-economic indicators, as well as a model to predict future emissions using a Holt Winters smoothing

Residual Adjustment to Increase Robustness in Linear Regression

An exploration of a novel method to increase robustness (resistance to outliers) in Linear Regression using adjustment of residual points as classified by Cook’s Distance. Adjustment level is a parameter (a function of outlier magnitude). Various functions are discussed, including Bayesian methods and domain knowledge approaches. Presented at UIUC Economics Research Symposium.

The Correlation Between Professor Ratings and Average Class GPA

An examination of the correlation between ratings of college professors from the website RateMyProfessor.com and GPA data across three major U.S public universities. We attempt to examine whether student perceptions (and consequently, class registration) are affected mainly by the average grade they received in the class. Submitted to 2015 SAS data convention

Using Bayesian Hierarchial Modeling to Inspect Gender Wage Gaps

Research extending the research done regarding the “gender wage gap” (statistically significant different between male and female employee salaries in industry) into academia using Bayesian Hierarchical Modeling. All academic employees from the state of Illinois were examined and data was separated by position, school and gender

Econometrics and Financial Engineering I and II

A lecture/talk series given to a crowd of ~40 students covering the statistical methods utilized in Financial Engineering and Econometrics. Topics covered include scraping of real-time financial data, stocks and bonds markets, statistical arbitrage, options pricing models, hedging, ARMA models and forecasting
